# SPCX × Trade Republic — Execution Mechanics for Selling an IPO Allocation on Day 1 (Block S4)

> Hub: [[spacex_ipo_market_map_handoff]] · [[COWORK]] · [[POLYMARKET_BRAIN]]

## Plain-English Summary

- **What this is.** Block S4 of the SPCX listing-day gameplan: desk research, from Trade Republic's (TR) published support pages, press releases, and terms only, into the broker mechanics of selling a SpaceX IPO allocation on day 1 (Friday 2026-06-12) — Alvaro's research items 1–10 and 16–17.
- **Why it exists.** Both sleeves of the gameplan terminate in sell tickets on TR. If TR can't stage a limit sell before the Nasdaq cross, charges hidden FX on the exit, or blocks day-1 flipping, the unwind plan changes materially — so every mechanic had to be pinned down or explicitly flagged for the human in-app check.
- **One-line takeaway.** TR is workable but minimal: market/limit/stop only (no trailing stop), limit validity 1 day or 360 days, partial sells fine (€1/order), trades execute **in EUR on LS Exchange** (FX is embedded in the spread, not itemized), a browser app exists as outage fallback, and **no day-1 flipping restriction appears anywhere in published terms** — but 6 items (pre-cross order staging, allocation booking time, fractional allocation handling, allocation FX rate, partial-fill fee semantics, flipping confirmation) are not in published docs and route to the in-app support chat, with the exact question list at the bottom.
- **Status.** Desk research done. The single highest-value remaining action is the **human D0 support chat** ([[spcx_listing_day_gameplan]] §6 calls it "the single highest-value 15 minutes left this week") — questions pre-ordered below.

## 1. Design — what was checked and how to read the table

**Scope.** Alvaro's research items 1–10 + 16–17, restated as 12 concrete questions: order types for US stocks; limit-order validity windows + minimum tick; partial sells; fractional-share handling for IPO allocations; pre-staging sell orders before the US open; real-time vs delayed in-app quotes; €1 fee semantics; FX conversion timing + spread; T+1 proceeds availability; outage fallbacks; and (items 16–17) any day-1 flipping or lock-up restriction on IPO allocations.

**Method.** TR's own support center (`support.traderepublic.com`), TR's IPO-feature press release (2026-06-06, the SpaceX-window launch), and TR's published pricing pages, supplemented by reputable broker-review sources (BrokerChooser, Curvo, BankerOnWheels, northern.finance) only where TR's own pages are silent. No in-app checks were performed — that is by design the human's job (D0 node of the gameplan).

**Confidence scale.** **High** = stated on a TR-owned page in plain words. **Medium** = stated by a reputable third-party review of TR, or on a TR page but generic (not US-stock- or IPO-specific). **Low** = inferred from venue mechanics; treat as a hypothesis. Anything that must be confirmed in-app before Friday carries **HUMAN-CHECK = YES** regardless of confidence.

**One structural fact that reframes several items.** TR is a single-venue broker: all stock orders execute on the **LS Exchange** (Lang & Schwarz, electronic system of the Hamburg Stock Exchange), **quoted and settled in EUR**, with Lang & Schwarz TradeCenter as sole market maker and Tradegate as the failover venue. There is no Nasdaq routing and no USD cash leg on a normal trade. Practical example: selling 20 SPCX shares "at $150" on TR actually means selling at the **EUR** bid L&S quotes (≈ $150 × EURUSD mirror of the Nasdaq price, minus their spread); the FX cost is inside that quote, never a separate line. This is also why [[spcx_listing_day_gameplan]] §5.3 "rule zero" (TR = order entry only, never a price feed) stands: you are trading against one market maker's EUR mirror of the US tape.

### Read

- **Nothing found kills the plan.** The two sleeves' unwind mechanics ([[spcx_listing_day_gameplan]] §5) survive: tranche sells are cheap (€1 each), limit-only discipline is supported, and the browser app is a real second screen for order entry.
- **The plan's quiet assumptions that desk research could NOT confirm** are exactly the dangerous ones: (i) that allocated shares sit in the account, sellable, before the cross; (ii) that a limit sell can rest on LS before the first Nasdaq print; (iii) that no flipping rule appears in the SPCX subscription fine print. All three are in-app questions only.
- **One mechanic is now pinned down and matters:** there is **no MOC order and no trailing stop**. The vntl-sleeve fallback (sell at/near the official close, §5.1) must be a hand-timed limit order in the last minutes — confirming the gameplan's existing "slippage vs official close is a real residual" caveat. And stop orders on a day-1 IPO tape are dangerous anyway (L&S EUR quotes gapping on a fast tape → stop becomes a market order at the next, possibly much worse, quote).
- **FX is a real but bounded leak.** Selling in EUR against L&S means paying their embedded EURUSD overlay (~25–100 bps third-party estimates) instead of an itemized fee. On a ~$20–25/share basis (~15%), a 0.5% FX/spread drag ≈ $0.7–0.8/share at a $150 exit — material to log, not material to the go/no-go. The *allocation* FX rate (item 9) is the bigger unknown because it shifts the cost basis of every share.

## 3. Practical example — one tranche, end to end

Suppose the Friday fill is 60 shares at $135 and the first print comes 18:30 CET at $165. The residual sleeve wants to sell 24 shares (40%) into strength. On TR that is: open SPCX in-app (or `app.traderepublic.com`), Sell → 24 shares → order type **Limit**, validity **1 day**, limit price entered in **EUR** — say the L&S bid mirrors $164.50 at EURUSD 1.08 → €152.30, so a 1–2-tick-inside limit is ~€152.20 (per §5.2's repeg-every-5-min rule). Cost: €1 if it executes. Proceeds: reinvestable immediately, withdrawable next day. What this example can NOT yet promise: that the 60 shares were in the account and sellable at 18:30 (item 6/12 HUMAN-CHECK), and the exact EUR debit you paid per share at allocation (item 9, Q4).

## 4. The in-app support question list (human task, D0 — ordered by decision impact)

Ask in this order; the first three change the plan if answered badly, the rest calibrate it. Phrase them as written — they are deliberately yes/no-able.

1. **"I subscribed to the SpaceX IPO. Once shares are allocated, can I sell them on the first trading day with no restriction — is there any flipping rule, minimum holding period, or penalty for selling day 1?"** *(items 16–17; a bad answer reroutes both sleeves)*
2. **"At what time on listing day will the allocated shares be booked into my account and become sellable — and will SPCX be quoted/tradable on LS Exchange before the Nasdaq opening cross, or only after the first US print?"** *(item 6; decides whether a resting limit sell can be staged Friday morning)*
3. **"Can my pro-rata allocation include a fractional share? If yes, does the fraction have to be sold separately as a market order?"** *(item 5)*
4. **"My subscription is in EUR but the offer price is $135. What EURUSD rate converts my debit, and at what moment is it fixed — subscription, allocation, or booking?"** *(item 9; moves the realized cost basis)*
5. **"On listing day, are there any special order-type restrictions on a fresh IPO listing — are limit and stop orders available from the first quote, or market orders only initially?"** *(item 1 corner case for day-1 listings)*
6. **"If the app and web interface are unreachable during trading hours, is there any way to place a sell order by phone?"** *(item 11; the published docs show no phone-order desk)*
7. **"What is the minimum price increment for a limit order on a US stock quoted in EUR — one cent?"** *(item 3; matters for the 1–2-ticks-inside-the-bid repeg rule)*
8. **"If a limit sell executes in several partial fills, is the €1 fee charged once per order or per execution?"** *(items 4/8 corner case)*

Log the answers (screenshot the chat) into this note the same day; the gameplan's D0 node treats Q1–Q2 as gating.
